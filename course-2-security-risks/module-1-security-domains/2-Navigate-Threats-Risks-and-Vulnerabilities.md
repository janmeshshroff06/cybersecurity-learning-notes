## Module 1.2: Navigate Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

## Overview

This section explains how to identify and manage threats, risks, and vulnerabilities, and how they impact organizations.

## Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

### Threat

- A potential danger that could harm systems or data  
- Examples:
  - Malware  
  - Hackers  
  - Insider threats  

### Vulnerability

- A weakness that can be exploited by a threat  
- Examples:
  - Weak passwords  
  - Outdated software  
  - Misconfigured systems  

### Risk

- The likelihood that a threat will exploit a vulnerability  

Key idea: risk increases when threats and vulnerabilities are both present  

## Key Insight

- Threats exploit vulnerabilities, creating risk  
- Reducing vulnerabilities lowers overall risk  

## Impacts of Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

### Ransomware

- Malware that encrypts data and locks systems  
- Attackers demand payment to restore access  

Impact:

- Stops operations  
- Causes downtime  
- Leads to financial loss  

### Layers of the Web

#### Surface Web

- Public websites (search engines, news sites)  

#### Deep Web

- Private systems (databases, company portals)  

#### Dark Web

- Hidden networks requiring special tools  
- Often used for:
  - Selling stolen data  
  - Communication between attackers  

### Financial Impact

- System downtime affects operations  
- Costs for recovery and repairs  
- Legal fines for non-compliance  

### Identity Theft

- Exposure of sensitive data (PII)  
- Data can be misused or sold  
- Impacts customers and employees  

### Reputational Damage

- Loss of customer trust  
- Negative public image  
- Long-term business impact  

## Managing Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

Organizations must:

- Identify risks early  
- Monitor systems continuously  
- Apply controls to reduce threats  

Analysts help by:

- Investigating threats  
- Responding to incidents  
- Improving security measures  

## NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

The NIST RMF provides a structured approach to managing risk.

### Steps

1. Prepare  
   - Identify possible threats  
   - Get ready to manage risks  

2. Categorize  
   - Classify systems based on importance  
   - Uses CIA Triad:
     - Confidentiality  
     - Integrity  
     - Availability  

3. Select  
   - Choose appropriate security controls  
   - Document security plans  

4. Implement  
   - Apply security measures  
   - Put controls into action  

5. Assess  
   - Test controls  
   - Identify weaknesses  

6. Authorize  
   - Approve system for operation  
   - Accept or reduce risk  

7. Monitor  
   - Continuously track security  
   - Update controls as needed  

## Key Insight

- RMF is a continuous cycle, not a one-time process  

## Best Practices

- Use strong passwords and authentication  
- Keep systems updated  
- Monitor logs and activity  
- Apply security controls  
- Train users to recognize threats  

## Key Takeaways

- Threats exploit vulnerabilities, creating risk  
- Cyberattacks can cause financial and reputational damage  
- RMF helps manage risk systematically  
- Continuous monitoring is essential  

## Big Picture

Cybersecurity is about identifying weaknesses, understanding threats, and reducing risk before damage occurs.
