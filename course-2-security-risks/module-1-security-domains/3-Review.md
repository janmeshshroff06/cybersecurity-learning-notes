## Overview

This section reviews the key concepts from Module 1 and reinforces your understanding of CISSP domains, threats, risks, vulnerabilities, and risk management frameworks.

## CISSP Security Domains Recap

- The CISSP domains provide a complete framework for cybersecurity  
- They cover key areas including:
  - Risk management  
  - Asset protection  
  - Network security  
  - Access control  
  - Security operations  

Key idea: all eight domains work together to protect organizations.

## Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities Recap

### Threat

- A potential danger to systems or data  

### Vulnerability

- A weakness that can be exploited  

### Risk

- The likelihood of a threat exploiting a vulnerability  

### Key Relationship

- Threat + Vulnerability = Risk  
- Reducing vulnerabilities lowers overall risk  

## Impact of Cyber Threats

### Ransomware

- Locks or encrypts data  
- Requires payment to restore access  

### Three Layers of the Web

- Surface Web: public content  
- Deep Web: private systems  
- Dark Web: hidden networks often used by attackers  

### Major Impacts on Organizations

- Financial loss  
- Data breaches  
- Identity theft  
- Reputational damage  

## NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) Recap

The NIST RMF helps organizations manage cybersecurity risk.

### Steps

1. Prepare – identify risks  
2. Categorize – classify systems  
3. Select – choose controls  
4. Implement – apply controls  
5. Assess – test effectiveness  
6. Authorize – approve system  
7. Monitor – continuous tracking  

## Key Insight

- RMF is a continuous cycle used to improve security over time
