## Module 4.2: Explore incident Reponse

---

## Overview

A **playbook** is a **structured guide** for responding to **security incidents** efficiently. It ensures that responses are:

* **Consistent**
* **Efficient**
* **Accurate**

Playbooks help security teams:

* **Identify** threats
* **Contain** incidents
* **Resolve** security issues

They work in tandem with **SIEM tools**, which trigger playbook actions based on alerts.

---

## Phases of an Incident Response Playbook

### 1. Initial Assessment

* **Objective:** Determine whether an alert is **legitimate** (e.g., a potential malware attack).

* **Actions:**

  * Analyze logs and metrics
  * Verify the validity of the threat

---

### 2. Containment

* **Objective:** **Limit damage** and stop the spread of the threat.

* **Actions:**

  * Isolate affected systems (e.g., disconnect compromised devices)

---

### 3. Elimination and Restoration

* **Objective:** **Remove** the threat and restore systems.

* **Actions:**

  * Eradicate malicious code
  * Use clean backups to restore systems

---

### 4. Post-Incident Activities

* **Objective:** Learn from the incident and improve security posture.

* **Actions:**

  * Document and report findings
  * Coordinate with relevant authorities or stakeholders

---

## Key Takeaways

* Playbooks guide **consistent, efficient**, and **accurate** responses during incidents.
* They ensure a **structured approach** for identifying, containing, and resolving threats.
* Playbooks **evolve** with new threats and insights.
