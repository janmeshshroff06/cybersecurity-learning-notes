# Module 3.1: Frameworks and Controls

## Overview

This section explains how organizations use security frameworks and controls to reduce risk and protect systems and data.

## What are Security Frameworks

Security frameworks are guidelines and best practices used to manage and reduce cybersecurity risk.

They help organizations:

- Identify risks  
- Protect systems  
- Detect threats  
- Respond to incidents  
- Recover from attacks  

## Common Framework Example

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

Core functions:

1. Identify – understand risks  
2. Protect – secure systems  
3. Detect – find threats  
4. Respond – take action  
5. Recover – restore operations  

## Secure Design (CIA Triad)

The CIA Triad is a fundamental concept in cybersecurity:

### Confidentiality

- Only authorized users can access data  
- Examples: passwords, encryption  

### Integrity

- Data remains accurate and unaltered  
- Examples: hashing, validation  

### Availability

- Systems and data are accessible when needed  
- Examples: backups, redundancy  

## Security Controls

Controls are safeguards used to protect systems.

### Types of Controls

Administrative controls:

- Policies and procedures  
- Training and awareness programs  

Technical controls:

- Firewalls  
- Encryption  
- Authentication systems  

Physical controls:

- Locks  
- Cameras  
- Security guards  

## Purpose of Controls

- Reduce risk  
- Prevent attacks  
- Detect suspicious activity  
- Support response and recovery  

## Compliance

Organizations must follow rules and regulations to protect data.

Examples:

- Legal requirements  
- Industry standards  
- Organizational policies  

Key idea: Compliance ensures organizations meet security expectations.

## Protecting Sensitive Data

- Identify sensitive information (PII, financial data)  
- Apply controls such as:
  - Encryption  
  - Access restrictions  
  - Secure storage  

## Key Takeaway

Frameworks and controls provide a structured way to manage cybersecurity risk.
