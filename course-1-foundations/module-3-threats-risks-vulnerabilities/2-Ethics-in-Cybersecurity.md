# Module 3.2: Ethics in Cybersecurity

## Overview

This section explains the importance of ethical behavior in cybersecurity. Cybersecurity professionals have access to sensitive systems and data, so their actions must follow strong ethical standards.

## Why Ethics Matter

Cybersecurity professionals:

- Have access to sensitive information  
- Can impact user privacy and safety  
- Play a critical role in protecting organizations  

Because of this, ethical behavior is essential.

## Core Ethical Principles

### Do No Harm

- Avoid actions that could negatively impact systems, users, or organizations  

### Respect Privacy

- Protect personal and sensitive information  
- Only access data when authorized  

### Act with Integrity

- Be honest and trustworthy  
- Avoid misuse of access or privileges  

### Follow Laws and Policies

- Comply with legal requirements  
- Follow organizational security policies  

## Ethical Decision-Making

When faced with a situation:

- Evaluate the potential impact of your actions  
- Follow established policies and guidelines  
- Choose actions that protect users and systems  

## Real-World Application

Ethics are applied in situations such as:

- Handling sensitive data responsibly  
- Reporting vulnerabilities instead of exploiting them  
- Maintaining confidentiality of systems and users  

## Importance of Ethics

- Builds trust between organizations and users  
- Prevents misuse of access  
- Supports professional responsibility  

## Key Takeaway

Ethics guide how cybersecurity professionals use their knowledge and access responsibly to protect systems, data, and people.
