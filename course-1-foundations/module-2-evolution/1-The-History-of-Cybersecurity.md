# Module 2.1: The History of Cybersecurity

## Overview

This section explains how cybersecurity has evolved alongside the growth of computers and the internet, and how past attacks have shaped modern security practices.

## Early Days of Computing (Pre-Internet)

- Computers were standalone systems  
- Used mainly by governments and large organizations  
- Very limited access  

### Security Context

- Security was not a major concern  
- No internet meant fewer external threats  

## The Rise of Networking

- Computers began connecting through networks  
- Led to the development of the internet  

### Impact

- Systems became more accessible  
- Increased exposure to threats  

## Early Cyber Attacks

- Mostly experimental or curiosity-driven  
- Examples:
  - Early computer viruses  
  - Unauthorized access attempts  

### Key Insight

Attackers were not always financially motivated.

## Expansion of the Internet

- Internet usage grew globally  
- Rapid increase in:
  - Users  
  - Data sharing  
  - Online services  

### Result

- Larger attack surface  
- More opportunities for cyberattacks  

## Modern Cyber Threats

Attacks became:

- More sophisticated  
- Organized  
- Financially motivated  

### Examples

- Ransomware  
- Phishing attacks  
- Data breaches  

## Evolution of Attackers

### Past

- Individual hackers  
- Motivated by curiosity or experimentation  

### Present

- Cybercriminal organizations  
- Nation-state attackers  
- Insider threats  

## Impact of Past Attacks

- Revealed system weaknesses  
- Led to development of:
  - Firewalls  
  - Antivirus software  
  - Encryption  

### Key Idea

Cybersecurity improved because of past failures.

## Common Attacks Over Time

- Viruses: spread between systems  
- Phishing: trick users into revealing data  
- Ransomware: lock data for payment  
- Denial-of-Service (DoS): overload systems  

## Incident Response

When responding to a security incident:

- Stay calm  
- Identify the issue  
- Contain the threat  
- Eliminate the cause  
- Recover systems  

## Key Takeaway

Cybersecurity continuously evolves because attackers constantly adapt.

## Real-World Application

These concepts are applied in real-world scenarios such as:

- Designing systems to defend against modern threats  
- Monitoring systems for suspicious activity  
- Responding to security incidents using structured processes  
- Improving defenses based on lessons from past attacks
