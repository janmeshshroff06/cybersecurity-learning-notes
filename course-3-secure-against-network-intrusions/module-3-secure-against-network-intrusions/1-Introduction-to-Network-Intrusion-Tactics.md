## Module 3.1: Introduction to Network Intrusion Tactics

## The Case for Securing Networks

## Overview

- Networks are constantly at risk of cyber attacks
- Attacks can compromise:
  - Data
  - Systems
  - Operations
- Securing networks is critical to protect:
  - Organizational assets
  - Reputation

## Common Network Threats

- Malware
  - Malicious software that damages or disrupts systems

- Spoofing
  - Pretending to be a trusted source

- Packet sniffing
  - Intercepting network data

- Packet flooding (DoS)
  - Overloading a network with traffic

## How Intrusions Compromise Systems

- Attackers exploit vulnerabilities to:
  - Gain unauthorized access
  - Steal data
  - Disrupt services

- Can occur through:
  - Weak security configurations
  - Unpatched systems
  - User mistakes

## Impact of Network Attacks

### Data Breaches

- Exposure of sensitive information

### Financial Loss

- Cost of recovery and damages

### Reputation Damage

- Loss of trust from users/customers

### Operational Disruption

- System downtime and reduced productivity

## Real-World Insight

- Large-scale breaches (e.g., retail companies) show:
  - Millions of records can be stolen
  - Attacks can go undetected for long periods

## Professional Perspective (Matt Insight)

- Security professionals must:
  - Detect threats early
  - Respond quickly
  - Continuously improve defenses

## Importance of Network Security

- Prevent unauthorized access
- Protect sensitive data
- Ensure system availability
- Maintain business continuity

## Key Takeaways

- Networks are vulnerable to multiple attack types
- Intrusions can cause serious financial and operational damage
- Understanding attack methods is critical for defense
- Strong security measures reduce risk and impact

## Big Picture

- Network security involves anticipating attacks, protecting systems, and minimizing damage
- Forms the foundation for defending against real-world cyber threats
