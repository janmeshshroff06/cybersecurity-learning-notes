# 2.1 Safeguard Information

## Security Controls

### Overview

* Organizations use **security controls** to protect assets and information from:

  * Theft
  * Unauthorized access
  * Data exposure
  * Misuse

* Security controls reduce risk before, during, and after security incidents.

* They help support the:

  * Confidentiality
  * Integrity
  * Availability (CIA) Triad

---

### What Are Security Controls?

* Security controls are safeguards designed to reduce security risks.

* They protect:

  * Information
  * Systems
  * Networks
  * Physical assets
  * Digital assets

---

### Types of Security Controls

Security controls are grouped into three categories:

#### Technical Controls

* Use technology to protect assets.

* Examples:

  * Encryption
  * Firewalls
  * Authentication systems
  * Antivirus software
  * Access controls

---

#### Operational Controls

* Performed by people.

* Support daily security operations.

* Examples:

  * Security awareness training
  * Incident response
  * Security monitoring
  * Daily procedures

---

#### Managerial Controls

* Focus on managing security programs and risk.

* Examples:

  * Policies
  * Standards
  * Procedures
  * Risk management plans

---

## Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)

### Overview

* The **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)** states that users should only receive the minimum access necessary to perform their job duties.

* Access should be:

  * Limited
  * Temporary when appropriate
  * Based on business needs

---

### Why Least Privilege Matters

* Supports the CIA Triad.

* Reduces risk of:

  * Data breaches
  * Unauthorized access
  * Accidental modification
  * Data loss

* Limits damage if an account is compromised.

---

### Common Account Types

#### Guest Accounts

* External users
* Customers
* Contractors
* Business partners

#### User Accounts

* Assigned to employees
* Based on job responsibilities

#### Service Accounts

* Used by applications and software

#### Privileged Accounts

* Administrative access
* Elevated permissions

---

### Privilege Creep

* Occurs when users accumulate permissions over time.

* Common causes:

  * Promotions
  * Department changes
  * New responsibilities

* Creates unnecessary security risk.

---

### Account Audits

#### Usage Audits

* Review resources users access.

* Identify unused permissions.

#### Privilege Audits

* Ensure permissions match job duties.

* Detect privilege creep.

#### Account Change Audits

* Review account modifications.

* Detect suspicious changes.

* Verify authorized activity.

---

## The Data Lifecycle

### Overview

* Data must be protected throughout its entire lifecycle.

* Security controls change depending on how data is being used.

* Five stages:

  1. Collect
  2. Store
  3. Use
  4. Archive
  5. Destroy

---

### Collect

* Data is gathered from internal or external sources.

---

### Store

* Data is saved for future use.

---

### Use

* Data is actively accessed and processed.

---

### Archive

* Data is retained for long-term storage.

---

### Destroy

* Data is securely removed when no longer needed.

---

## Data Governance

### Overview

* Data governance is a structured process for managing information.

* Ensures data remains:

  * Private
  * Accurate
  * Available
  * Secure throughout its lifecycle.

---

### Data Governance Roles

#### Data Owner

Responsible for deciding:

* Who can access data
* Who can edit data
* Who can use data
* Who can destroy data

---

#### Data Custodian

Responsible for:

* Storage
* Transportation
* Protection
* Safe handling of information

---

#### Data Steward

Responsible for:

* Implementing governance policies
* Maintaining governance standards
* Supporting compliance efforts

---

## Information Privacy

### Overview

* Information privacy protects data from:

  * Unauthorized access
  * Unauthorized distribution

* Focuses on:

  * How data is shared
  * Who can access data
  * When access is allowed

---

### Privacy vs Security

#### Information Privacy

* Gives individuals control over their personal information.

* Determines how information is collected and shared.

#### Information Security (InfoSec)

* Protects information from threats.

* Keeps data secure in all states:

  * At rest
  * In transit
  * In use

---

### Why Privacy Matters

* Builds customer trust.

* Protects personal information.

* Reduces misuse of data.

* Helps organizations maintain compliance.

---

## Legally Protected Information

### PII (Personally Identifiable Information)

* Information that can identify or locate a person.

* Examples:

  * Name
  * Address
  * Phone number
  * Email address

---

### PHI (Protected Health Information)

* Health-related information protected by law.

* Protected under HIPAA in the United States.

---

### SPII (Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information)

* More sensitive form of PII.

* Examples:

  * Bank account numbers
  * Login credentials
  * Financial information
  * Social Security numbers

---

## Major Privacy Regulations

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

* European Union privacy law.

* Gives individuals control over personal information.

* Applies to organizations handling EU resident data.

---

### PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)

* Security standard for protecting payment card data.

* Helps prevent fraud and theft.

---

### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)

* U.S. law protecting patient health information.

* Prevents unauthorized disclosure of medical records.

---

## Security Audits and Assessments

### Security Audit

* Reviews:

  * Policies
  * Procedures
  * Security controls

* Measures compliance with regulations and standards.

---

### Security Assessment

* Evaluates how effective security controls are against threats.

* Identifies weaknesses and improvement opportunities.

---

### Audit vs Assessment

| Audit | Assessment |
|---------|---------|
| Focuses on compliance | Focuses on security effectiveness |
| Usually annual | Usually every 3–6 months |
| Reviews controls and policies | Tests security posture |

---

## Cybersecurity Importance

* Security controls protect organizational assets and sensitive data.

* Least privilege reduces unauthorized access.

* Data governance ensures proper handling of information.

* Privacy regulations protect individuals' personal data.

* Audits and assessments help organizations maintain strong security programs.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Security controls reduce security risks and protect information.

* The three main control categories are:

  * Technical
  * Operational
  * Managerial

* The Principle of Least Privilege limits access to only what users need.

* The data lifecycle includes:

  * Collect
  * Store
  * Use
  * Archive
  * Destroy

* Data governance assigns responsibilities to:

  * Owners
  * Custodians
  * Stewards

* Important protected data types include:

  * PII
  * PHI
  * SPII

* Major privacy regulations include:

  * GDPR
  * PCI DSS
  * HIPAA

* Security audits and assessments help organizations maintain compliance and security.

---

## Big Picture

Protecting information requires more than technology alone. Organizations use security controls, least privilege, data governance, privacy regulations, and ongoing audits to ensure sensitive information remains secure, private, and available throughout its entire lifecycle.
