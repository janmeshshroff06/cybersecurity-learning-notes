# **2.4 Review: Protect Organizational Assets**

---

## **Overview**

* Module 2 focused on protecting organizational assets through:

  * Security controls
  * Information privacy
  * Data governance
  * Encryption
  * Access management

* The goal is to ensure information remains:

  * Confidential
  * Accurate
  * Available

* Security professionals use these concepts to reduce risk and protect sensitive information.

---

## **Security Controls**

### **Purpose**

* Security controls are safeguards designed to reduce security risks.

* Help organizations protect:

  * Systems
  * Networks
  * Information
  * Physical assets

---

### **Three Categories**

#### **Technical Controls**

* Encryption
* Firewalls
* Authentication systems
* Security software

#### **Operational Controls**

* Security awareness training
* Incident response
* Monitoring
* Daily security procedures

#### **Managerial Controls**

* Policies
* Standards
* Procedures
* Risk management activities

---

## **Information Privacy and Data Protection**

### **Information Privacy**

* Protects information from:

  * Unauthorized access
  * Unauthorized disclosure

* Gives individuals control over personal information.

---

### **Protected Information Types**

#### **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**

* Names
* Addresses
* Email addresses
* Phone numbers

#### **SPII (Sensitive Personally Identifiable Information)**

* Social Security numbers
* Financial information
* Login credentials

#### **PHI (Protected Health Information)**

* Medical records
* Health-related information

---

### **Data Governance**

* Structured approach to managing data.

* Ensures data remains:

  * Secure
  * Accurate
  * Available
  * Compliant

---

### **Key Data Roles**

#### **Data Owner**

* Determines:

  * Access permissions
  * Usage permissions
  * Modification permissions

#### **Data Custodian**

* Responsible for:

  * Storage
  * Protection
  * Transportation
  * Safe handling

#### **Data Steward**

* Implements governance policies.

* Supports compliance efforts.

---

## **The Data Lifecycle**

### **Five Stages**

1. Collect
2. Store
3. Use
4. Archive
5. Destroy

---

### **Importance**

* Security controls must protect data during every stage.

* Different lifecycle stages require different protections.

---

## **Authentication, Authorization, and Accounting (AAA)**

### **Authentication**

* Verifies identity.

* Answers:

  * "Who are you?"

---

### **Authorization**

* Determines permissions.

* Answers:

  * "What are you allowed to do?"

---

### **Accounting**

* Tracks activity.

* Records:

  * User actions
  * Access attempts
  * Resource usage

---

### **Identity and Access Management (IAM)**

* Manages:

  * User identities
  * Access permissions
  * Authentication processes

---

## **Security Principles**

### **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)**

* Users receive only the access necessary to perform their jobs.

---

### **Benefits**

* Reduces:

  * Insider threats
  * Accidental misuse
  * Unauthorized access

---

### **Separation of Duties**

* Responsibilities are divided among multiple people.

* Reduces:

  * Fraud
  * Abuse
  * Human error

---

## **Authentication Technologies**

### **Single Sign-On (SSO)**

* One login provides access to multiple systems.

---

### **Benefits**

* Convenience
* Productivity
* Reduced password fatigue

---

### **Risk**

* One compromised account may affect multiple systems.

---

### **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**

* Requires two or more authentication factors.

---

### **Authentication Factors**

#### **Knowledge**

* Passwords
* PINs

#### **Ownership**

* Tokens
* Mobile devices
* OTPs

#### **Characteristic**

* Biometrics
* Fingerprints
* Facial recognition

---

## **Encryption and Cryptography**

### **Cryptography**

* Protects sensitive information through mathematical processes.

---

### **Encryption**

* Converts plaintext into ciphertext.

---

### **Decryption**

* Converts ciphertext back into plaintext.

---

### **Cryptographic Keys**

* Used to:

  * Encrypt data
  * Decrypt data

---

### **Symmetric Encryption**

* Uses one shared key.

* Faster.

* Efficient for large amounts of data.

---

### **Asymmetric Encryption**

* Uses:

  * Public key
  * Private key

* More secure.

* Used for secure communications.

---

### **Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)**

* Framework that combines:

  * Encryption
  * Identity verification
  * Digital certificates

* Establishes trust online.

---

### **Digital Certificates**

* Verify the identity of public key owners.

---

### **Certificate Authorities (CAs)**

* Trusted organizations that issue digital certificates.

---

## **Hashing and Integrity**

### **Hash Functions**

* One-way algorithms that create unique outputs.

---

### **Hash Values**

* Digital fingerprints of data.

---

### **Uses**

* Verify integrity.
* Detect file modifications.
* Support investigations.

---

### **SHA-256**

* Common modern hashing algorithm.

---

### **Non-Repudiation**

* Prevents users from denying actions they performed.

* Supports authenticity and accountability.

---

## **Security Monitoring**

### **Access Logs**

* Record:

  * User activity
  * Login attempts
  * Resource access

---

### **Sessions**

* User interactions from login to logout.

---

### **Session IDs**

* Unique identifiers used to track sessions.

---

### **Session Cookies**

* Maintain authenticated sessions.

---

### **Session Hijacking**

* Occurs when attackers steal valid session identifiers.

* Allows attackers to impersonate legitimate users.

---

## **Privacy and Compliance Regulations**

### **GDPR**

* European Union privacy regulation.

* Gives individuals greater control over personal information.

---

### **HIPAA**

* Protects health information in the United States.

---

### **PCI DSS**

* Protects payment card information.

---

## **Cybersecurity Importance**

* Security controls reduce risk.
* Access controls protect organizational resources.
* Encryption protects confidentiality.
* Hashing protects integrity.
* IAM manages identities and permissions.
* Logging and accounting help detect and investigate threats.
* Privacy regulations protect sensitive information and support compliance.

---

## **Key Takeaways**

* Security controls protect organizational assets and reduce risk.

* Data governance manages information throughout the data lifecycle.

* AAA consists of:

  * Authentication
  * Authorization
  * Accounting

* MFA provides stronger security than passwords alone.

* PoLP and Separation of Duties help prevent misuse of access.

* Cryptography protects confidentiality through encryption.

* PKI establishes trust using certificates and key pairs.

* Hashing supports integrity and non-repudiation.

* Access logs and monitoring support incident investigations.

* Compliance regulations help protect sensitive information.

---

## **Big Picture**

Protecting organizational assets requires multiple layers of security working together. Security controls, data governance, encryption, authentication, authorization, monitoring, and privacy regulations all contribute to safeguarding information. By combining these controls, organizations can reduce risk, maintain compliance, and protect their most valuable assets from cyber threats.
