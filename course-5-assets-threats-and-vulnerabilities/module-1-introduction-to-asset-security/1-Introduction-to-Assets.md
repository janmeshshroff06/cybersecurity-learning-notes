## Module 5.1: Introduction to Assets

## The What, Why, and How of Asset Security

## Overview

- Organizations must protect valuable assets from threats and vulnerabilities
- Security professionals analyze:
  - Assets
  - Threats
  - Vulnerabilities
  - Risks
- Asset security helps protect:
  - Confidentiality
  - Integrity
  - Availability (CIA Triad)

## Security Planning

### Purpose

- Organizations create security plans to:
  - Protect valuable resources
  - Reduce uncertainty
  - Minimize risks
  - Maintain operations

### Risk

- A risk is anything that can negatively impact:
  - Confidentiality
  - Integrity
  - Availability

- Security teams work to reduce risks affecting important assets

## The CIA Triad

### Confidentiality

- Ensures information is only accessible to authorized users

### Integrity

- Ensures information remains accurate and trustworthy

### Availability

- Ensures information and systems remain accessible when needed

### Why It Matters

- The CIA Triad is the foundation of information security
- Security controls are designed to protect all three principles

## Assets

### What is an Asset?

- An asset is anything that has value to an organization

### Examples of Assets

- People
- Buildings
- Equipment
- Data
- Information systems
- Intellectual property

### Asset Prioritization

- Not all assets have equal value
- Organizations must:
  - Identify critical assets
  - Prioritize protection efforts
  - Allocate security resources effectively

## Threats

### What is a Threat?

- A threat is any circumstance or event that can harm an asset

### Types of Threats

#### Intentional

- Hackers
- Theft
- Sabotage

#### Accidental

- Human error
- Misconfigurations

#### Environmental

- Floods
- Fires
- Severe weather

## Vulnerabilities

### What is a Vulnerability?

- A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat

### Examples

- Weak passwords
- Outdated software
- Misconfigured systems
- Poor physical security

## Assets, Threats, and Vulnerabilities

### Relationship

- Assets are:
  - What organizations want to protect

- Threats are:
  - What can cause harm

- Vulnerabilities are:
  - Weaknesses that threats exploit

### Security Risk Analysis

- Security teams evaluate:
  - Valuable assets
  - Potential threats
  - Existing vulnerabilities

- This process helps identify and manage risk

## Security Mindset

### Overview

- Security professionals continuously think about:
  - Potential threats
  - Weaknesses
  - Risk reduction strategies

### Goal

- Identify problems before they become security incidents

## Security Starts with Asset Classification

### Overview

- Organizations must know what assets they have before they can protect them
- Effective security begins with:
  - Asset management
  - Asset inventories
  - Asset classification

## Asset Management

### Definition

- Asset management is the process of:
  - Tracking assets
  - Tracking risks affecting assets

### Importance

- Organizations can only protect assets they know exist
- Asset management supports:
  - Security planning
  - Risk management
  - Resource allocation

## Asset Inventory

### Definition

- An asset inventory is a catalog of assets that require protection

### Benefits

- Tracks valuable resources
- Identifies missing assets
- Improves visibility
- Supports security planning

## Asset Classification

### Definition

- Asset classification is the process of labeling assets based on:
  - Sensitivity
  - Importance

### Purpose

- Determines:
  - Protection requirements
  - Access permissions
  - Handling procedures

## Common Classification Levels

### Public

- Information that can be shared with anyone
- Lowest protection requirements

### Internal-Only

- Information intended for use within the organization
- Should not be shared externally

### Confidential

- Information limited to authorized individuals or teams
- Requires stronger protection

### Restricted

- Highest classification level
- Access is granted on a strict need-to-know basis

### Examples of Restricted Data

- Intellectual property
- Health records
- Payment information
- Trade secrets

## Need-to-Know Principle

### Overview

- Access should only be provided to individuals who require information to perform their job duties

### Benefits

- Reduces unnecessary exposure
- Protects sensitive information
- Supports least-privilege access

## Why Asset Classification Matters

### Benefits

- Supports risk management
- Improves security planning
- Guides access control decisions
- Helps determine:
  - Who can view information
  - Who can modify information
  - Who can delete information

## Continuous Asset Management

### Overview

- Asset management is an ongoing process

### Purpose

- Discover security gaps
- Identify new risks
- Maintain accurate inventories
- Improve organizational security

## Key Takeaways

- Assets are valuable resources organizations must protect
- Risks result from threats exploiting vulnerabilities
- The CIA Triad focuses on confidentiality, integrity, and availability
- Asset management helps organizations track and protect valuable resources
- Asset inventories provide visibility into assets requiring protection
- Asset classification determines how assets should be protected
- Common classification levels include Public, Internal-Only, Confidential, and Restricted
- The need-to-know principle limits access to authorized personnel
- Effective asset security begins with identifying, classifying, and managing assets

## Big Picture

- Asset security is the foundation of cybersecurity
- Organizations must identify what they value, understand the threats and vulnerabilities that could affect those assets, and classify them appropriately
- By managing and protecting assets according to their importance, organizations can better reduce risk and maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of critical information
