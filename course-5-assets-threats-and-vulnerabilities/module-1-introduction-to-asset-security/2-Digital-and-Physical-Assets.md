# **5.2 Digital and Physical Assets**

---

## **Assets in a Digital World**

---

### **Overview**

* Organizations rely on both:

  * Physical assets
  * Digital assets

* As technology evolves, digital assets have become some of the most valuable resources organizations own.

* Security professionals must protect assets regardless of whether they exist in:

  * Physical form
  * Digital form

---

## **Physical Assets**

---

### **Definition**

* Physical assets are tangible items that have value to an organization.

---

### **Examples**

* Buildings
* Office equipment
* Computers
* Mobile devices
* Servers
* Networking equipment
* Security cameras

---

### **Security Considerations**

* Physical assets require protection from:

  * Theft
  * Damage
  * Unauthorized access
  * Natural disasters

---

## **Digital Assets**

---

### **Definition**

* Digital assets are assets that exist in electronic form.

---

### **Examples**

* Customer information
* Employee records
* Databases
* Intellectual property
* Software
* Digital documents
* Cloud-stored information

---

### **Importance**

* Digital assets often contain:

  * Sensitive information
  * Proprietary business data
  * Financial information

* Loss or compromise can significantly impact an organization.

---

## **States of Data**

---

### **Overview**

* Data exists in three primary states:

  * Data at rest
  * Data in transit
  * Data in use

---

### **Data at Rest**

* Data stored on a device and not actively being accessed.

---

### **Examples**

* Files on a hard drive
* Archived records
* Database storage
* Cloud storage

---

### **Security Controls**

* Encryption
* Access controls
* Backup procedures

---

### **Data in Transit**

* Data moving from one location to another.

---

### **Examples**

* Email transmissions
* Web traffic
* File transfers
* Network communications

---

### **Security Controls**

* TLS/SSL encryption
* VPNs
* Secure communication protocols

---

### **Data in Use**

* Data actively being accessed, processed, or modified.

---

### **Examples**

* Editing documents
* Running applications
* Querying databases

---

### **Security Controls**

* Authentication
* Authorization
* Endpoint protection

---

## **The Emergence of Cloud Security**

---

### **Overview**

* Organizations increasingly store assets in cloud environments.

* Cloud computing provides:

  * Scalability
  * Flexibility
  * Accessibility

---

## **Cloud Computing**

---

### **Definition**

* Cloud computing provides access to computing resources over the internet.

---

### **Resources Provided**

* Storage
* Processing power
* Applications
* Databases

---

### **Benefits**

* Reduced infrastructure costs
* Increased availability
* Improved scalability
* Easier collaboration

---

## **Cloud Assets**

---

### **Examples**

* Cloud databases
* Cloud applications
* Virtual machines
* Backups
* Customer records
* Business documents

---

### **Why Protection Matters**

* Cloud assets often contain sensitive organizational information.

* Misconfigured cloud resources can expose:

  * Customer data
  * Financial records
  * Intellectual property

---

## **Shared Responsibility Model**

---

### **Overview**

* Cloud security is shared between:

  * Cloud service providers
  * Organizations using cloud services

---

### **Provider Responsibilities**

* Physical infrastructure
* Hardware maintenance
* Core cloud services

---

### **Organization Responsibilities**

* Data protection
* User access management
* Security configurations
* Compliance requirements

---

## **Asset Protection Strategies**

---

### **Common Security Controls**

* Asset inventories
* Asset classification
* Encryption
* Access controls
* Monitoring and logging
* Backup and recovery plans

---

### **Purpose**

* Reduce risk
* Protect valuable resources
* Maintain business continuity

---

## **Key Takeaways**

* Organizations manage both physical and digital assets.

* Physical assets include hardware, buildings, and equipment.

* Digital assets include data, software, databases, and cloud resources.

* Data exists in three states:

  * At rest
  * In transit
  * In use

* Cloud computing has expanded the number of digital assets organizations must protect.

* Cloud security follows a shared responsibility model.

* Asset protection requires classification, access controls, encryption, and continuous monitoring.

---

## **Big Picture**

Modern organizations depend heavily on both physical and digital assets. Security professionals must understand how data moves through different states, how cloud technologies affect asset management, and how to apply security controls that protect valuable organizational resources from threats and vulnerabilities.
