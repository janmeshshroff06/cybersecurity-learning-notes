# Review: Introduction to Asset Security

## Module 1 Summary

### What You Learned

* Security planning helps organizations:

  * Protect valuable assets
  * Reduce uncertainty
  * Manage risks

* The CIA Triad consists of:

  * Confidentiality
  * Integrity
  * Availability

* Assets are anything valuable to an organization.

* Threats are events or circumstances that can harm assets.

* Vulnerabilities are weaknesses that threats can exploit.

---

## Asset Management and Classification

### Key Concepts

* Asset management involves:

  * Tracking assets
  * Tracking risks that affect assets

* Asset inventories help organizations:

  * Identify assets
  * Track resources
  * Prioritize protection efforts

* Common asset classifications include:

  * Public
  * Internal-only
  * Confidential
  * Restricted

* The need-to-know principle limits access to only those who require information to perform their job duties.

---

## Digital and Physical Assets

### Key Concepts

* Physical assets include:

  * Buildings
  * Computers
  * Servers
  * Network devices

* Digital assets include:

  * Databases
  * Customer information
  * Intellectual property
  * Digital documents

* Data exists in three states:

  * Data at rest
  * Data in transit
  * Data in use

* Cloud computing allows organizations to store and access resources remotely while introducing new security considerations.

---

## Risk and Asset Security

### Key Concepts

* Security plans are built around:

  * Policies
  * Standards
  * Procedures

* Security is a shared responsibility involving:

  * People
  * Processes
  * Technology

* Compliance helps organizations follow:

  * Internal standards
  * External regulations

* The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) consists of:

  * Core
  * Tiers
  * Profiles

---

## NIST CSF Core Functions

### Five Functions

* Identify
* Protect
* Detect
* Respond
* Recover

---

## Key Takeaways

* Assets, threats, and vulnerabilities form the foundation of risk management.
* Organizations can only protect assets they know about and track.
* Asset classification helps determine how information should be protected.
* Data security depends on protecting data at rest, in transit, and in use.
* Security plans rely on policies, standards, and procedures.
* The NIST Cybersecurity Framework provides a structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks.
* Effective cybersecurity begins with understanding and protecting organizational assets.

---

## Big Picture

Asset security starts with identifying what is valuable to an organization and understanding the risks that could impact it. By managing assets, classifying information appropriately, following security plans, and using frameworks such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, organizations can reduce risk, improve resilience, and better protect their physical and digital resources.
