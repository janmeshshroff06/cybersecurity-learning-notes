# **4.4 Threat Modeling**

---

## **A Proactive Approach to Security**

---

### **Overview**

* **Proactive security** focuses on preventing attacks before they happen.
* Instead of waiting for incidents, organizations work to:

  * Identify risks early
  * Reduce vulnerabilities
  * Strengthen defenses
* One of the primary proactive security techniques is:

  * **Threat modeling**
* Threat modeling helps organizations:

  * Identify valuable assets
  * Predict possible attacks
  * Develop effective security controls

---

### **Proactive vs. Reactive Security**

* **Proactive security**

  * Prevents attacks before they occur
  * Identifies weaknesses early
  * Improves security planning

* **Reactive security**

  * Responds after an attack occurs
  * Investigates incidents
  * Restores systems and services

---

### **Threat Modeling**

* **Threat modeling** is the process of:

  * Identifying assets
  * Identifying threats
  * Identifying vulnerabilities
  * Evaluating possible attack paths
  * Planning appropriate defenses

* Helps answer questions such as:

  * What needs protection?
  * Who might attack it?
  * How could they attack?
  * What would the impact be?
  * How can the risk be reduced?

---

### **Purpose of Threat Modeling**

* Helps organizations:

  * Detect weaknesses before attackers do
  * Prioritize security risks
  * Design stronger security controls
  * Reduce the attack surface
  * Improve security architecture

---

### **Thinking Like an Attacker**

* Security professionals analyze systems from an attacker's perspective.

* They ask:

  * What assets are valuable?
  * What vulnerabilities exist?
  * Which attack vectors are available?
  * Which defenses are weakest?

---

### **Benefits of Threat Modeling**

* Improves:

  * Risk management
  * Security planning
  * Resource allocation
  * Incident prevention

* Helps organizations:

  * Fix vulnerabilities early
  * Reduce security costs
  * Build more secure systems

---

### **Continuous Process**

* Threat modeling is not:

  * A one-time activity

* Organizations should continually:

  * Review assets
  * Evaluate new threats
  * Update defenses
  * Reassess risks as systems change

---

## **PASTA: Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis**

---

### **Overview**

* **PASTA** stands for:

  * **Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis**

* It is:

  * A risk-centered threat modeling framework

* Designed to help organizations:

  * Understand business risks
  * Analyze vulnerabilities
  * Simulate realistic attacks
  * Prioritize mitigation efforts

---

### **Why Use PASTA?**

* Connects:

  * Business objectives
  * Technical systems
  * Security risks

* Helps security teams:

  * Make informed security decisions
  * Focus on the highest-risk vulnerabilities
  * Improve overall security posture

---

### **The Seven Stages of PASTA**

---

#### **Stage 1: Define Business and Security Objectives**

* Identify:

  * Business goals
  * Critical assets
  * Security objectives

* Determine:

  * What needs protection
  * Why it is important

---

#### **Stage 2: Define the Technical Scope**

* Identify:

  * Applications
  * Networks
  * Systems
  * Technologies
  * Infrastructure

* Define what will be evaluated.

---

#### **Stage 3: Application Decomposition**

* Break the application into components.

* Analyze:

  * Data flow
  * User interactions
  * Trust boundaries
  * System architecture

* Helps reveal:

  * Weak points
  * Potential attack surfaces

---

#### **Stage 4: Threat Analysis**

* Identify:

  * Threat actors
  * Attack vectors
  * Attack techniques
  * Possible attack scenarios

* Determine how attackers may compromise the system.

---

#### **Stage 5: Weakness and Vulnerability Analysis**

* Examine:

  * Existing vulnerabilities
  * Security gaps
  * Misconfigurations
  * Missing controls

* Determine:

  * How attackers could exploit weaknesses

---

#### **Stage 6: Attack Modeling and Simulation**

* Simulate realistic attacks.

* Evaluate:

  * Exploitation methods
  * Attack paths
  * Potential impact

* Allows organizations to understand how attacks would unfold.

---

#### **Stage 7: Risk Analysis and Management**

* Evaluate:

  * Likelihood
  * Severity
  * Business impact

* Prioritize:

  * Remediation efforts
  * Security improvements

* Recommend:

  * Appropriate security controls
  * Risk mitigation strategies

---

### **Benefits of PASTA**

* Helps organizations:

  * Prioritize the most serious risks
  * Improve threat detection
  * Strengthen security planning
  * Better allocate security resources
  * Build stronger defenses before attacks occur

---

### **Risk-Centered Approach**

* PASTA focuses on:

  * Business risk
  * Technical risk

* Encourages organizations to:

  * Address vulnerabilities with the greatest potential impact first

---

### **Threat Modeling Best Practices**

* Keep threat models:

  * Updated regularly
  * Focused on critical assets
  * Based on realistic threats
  * Aligned with business objectives

* Include:

  * Diverse security teams
  * Developers
  * System administrators
  * Business stakeholders

* Different perspectives help identify threats that one team might overlook.

---

## **Key Takeaways**

* Proactive security focuses on preventing attacks before they occur.

* Threat modeling identifies assets, threats, vulnerabilities, attack paths, and security controls before incidents happen.

* Security professionals improve defenses by thinking like attackers.

* PASTA is a risk-centered threat modeling framework consisting of seven stages:

  * Define business objectives
  * Define technical scope
  * Application decomposition
  * Threat analysis
  * Vulnerability analysis
  * Attack simulation
  * Risk analysis and management

* PASTA combines business goals with technical analysis to prioritize the highest security risks.

* Threat modeling is an ongoing process that strengthens an organization's overall cybersecurity posture.

---

## **Big Picture**

Threat modeling helps organizations move from reacting to cyberattacks to preventing them. By identifying valuable assets, analyzing vulnerabilities, simulating realistic attacks, and prioritizing risks before attackers exploit them, organizations can make smarter security decisions. Frameworks like **PASTA** provide a structured, business-focused approach to understanding threats, strengthening defenses, and continuously improving an organization's security posture as technologies and threats evolve.
